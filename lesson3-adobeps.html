Introduction to the Color Adjustment

An adjustment layer alters the color and tone of your image without permanently modifying the pixel values. You may, for example, make a Levels or Curves adjustment layer instead of applying a Levels or Curves modification straight to your image. Color and tone adjustments are saved in the adjustment layer and applied to all levels below it; you may modify many layers with a single adjustment rather than altering each layer individually. At any point, you may undo your modifications and restore the original image.
The tools are easy to access and use. The interface is also customizable so you can change it to fit your needs.

More Information

Fill layers allow you to fill an entire layer with a single color, a gradient, or a pattern. Fill layers, unlike adjustment layers, have no effect on the layers underneath them. It lets you do:

i. Edits that are not destructive - At any moment, you may experiment with alternative settings and re-edit the adjustment layer. You may also lessen the effect of the modification by reducing the layer's opacity.

ii. Edits that are selective. - To make an adjustment to a portion of a picture, paint on the image mask of the adjustment layer. By re-editing the layer mask, you may later decide which sections of the image are modified. You may change the adjustment by painting different gray tones on the mask.

iii. Edits to make changes to several photos. – To apply the same color and tone modifications to many photographs, copy and paste adjustment layers.

Gradient: How To Use
Click the gradient to display the Gradient Editor, or click the inverted arrow and choose a gradient from the pop‑up panel. Set additional options if desired.

Gradient: Options

a. Style specifies the shape of the gradient.

b. Angle specifies the angle at which the gradient is applied.

c. Scale changes the size of the gradient.

d. Reverse flips the orientation of the gradient.

e. Dither reduces banding by applying dithering to the gradient.

f. Align With Layer uses the bounding box of the layer to calculate the gradient fill. You can drag in the image window to move the center of the gradient.

Remember these terms

The special terms you need to remember are:

1. ADJUSTMENT LAYER
- alters the color and tone of your image without permanently modifying the pixel values.

2. GRADIENT
- helps user to make subtle color and light shifts over an image.

3. GRADIENT OPTIONS
- includes style, angle, scale, reverse, dither, and align
